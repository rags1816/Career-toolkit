# Career Toolkit — Methodology

## Origin

Developed by Vijay L Narasimhan, 2025–26, drawing on 30+ years of senior
procurement/product career progression (BP, Shell, NESO) and the discipline
of writing evidence-based, criterion-mapped job applications for senior
public and private sector roles.

## Problem this solves

Most job application advice focuses on generic phrasing ("results-driven,"
"strategic thinker") rather than matching specific, provable career evidence
to the specific competency criteria a role is being assessed against. Senior
applications (especially public sector, criterion-scored formats) reward
precise evidence-to-criterion mapping — and penalise vague or overclaimed
language. This toolkit structures that mapping process and encourages honest
acknowledgment of genuine gaps rather than overclaiming.

## Built on / credited frameworks

- **Framework: Competency-based / STAR interview and application methodology**
  (widely used in UK public sector recruitment, e.g. Civil Service Success
  Profiles)
  - What it contributes: the general practice of mapping past experience to
    named criteria using Situation-Task-Action-Result structure
  - How this tool adapts it: builds a structured, reusable "evidence bank"
    (e.g. Castrol India warehouse network reduction, BP category savings)
    that can be matched against different criteria sets across multiple
    applications, rather than rewritten from scratch each time

## Core framework (your original model)

The toolkit organises career evidence into a structured bank, tagged by the
type of achievement it demonstrates (e.g. transformation/change, financial
value delivered, innovation, data/product thinking, stakeholder influence).
When applying to a role, the relevant criteria (e.g. PBMG, PROD, INOV, or a
role's specific competency framework) are matched against the evidence bank
to surface the strongest fit — and, importantly, to flag where a genuine gap
exists (e.g. B2B vs. consumer product experience) so it can be addressed
honestly rather than concealed.

## Why this approach (rationale)

Evidence-grounded, honest positioning produces stronger, more defensible
applications than generic or overclaimed language — particularly for senior
and criterion-scored roles where assessors are trained to spot vague claims.
Reusing a structured evidence bank across applications also saves significant
time versus rebuilding evidence narratives from scratch each time.
